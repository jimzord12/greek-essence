## 0. Document Purpose and Authority

This Product Requirements Document defines **what Greek Essence is, why the product exists, who it serves, what the initial release must accomplish, and which product requirements govern its design and implementation**.

It is the canonical source of truth for product decisions. It is intended for the business owner, product contributors, content creators, designers, developers, marketers, external partners, and AI agents working on the project.

This document does not prescribe the final visual design or implementation architecture. Those concerns belong to the following downstream documents:

- **Prototype Specification:** page composition, component variants, responsive behavior, interactions, visual system, motion, and prototype acceptance criteria.
- **Technical Design:** framework, architecture, data models, integrations, deployment, testing, security implementation, analytics implementation, and operational tooling.

Where these documents conflict, the Product Requirements Document governs product intent. A downstream document may clarify or implement a requirement, but it may not silently change the product.

### 0.1 How to Use This Document

Contributors should use this PRD to answer:

- What problem is Greek Essence solving?
- Which visitors matter most?
- What makes the offer distinctive?
- What is included in the MVP?
- What is explicitly excluded?
- What should each page and reusable product component accomplish?
- How should success be measured?
- Which decisions are settled, assumed, or still open?

### 0.2 Requirement Language

The words **must**, **should**, and **may** have deliberate meanings:

- **Must:** required for MVP acceptance unless explicitly deferred by the product owner.
- **Should:** strongly recommended and expected unless a documented constraint justifies omission.
- **May:** optional, future-facing, or dependent on validation.

### 0.3 Supersession

After this PRD is reviewed and approved, it supersedes the original UX Strategy & Website Requirements Brief as the primary product reference. The original brief should be archived rather than immediately deleted, so its discovery history remains available.

### Key Decisions

- This PRD governs product intent.
- Prototype and technical decisions must trace back to this document.
- Unknown business facts remain open decisions; they are not to be invented by designers, developers, or agents.
