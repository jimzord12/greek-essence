## 14. Conversion Strategy

### 14.1 CTA Hierarchy

**Primary global CTA:** `Plan My Trip`  
**Trip-request page title:** `Request a Custom Itinerary`  
**Secondary CTA:** `Book a Consultation`  
**Support actions:** email, WhatsApp, phone, and Viber if actively monitored

The primary CTA label must remain consistent across navigation, hero sections, destination pages, experience pages, and journey pages.

### 14.2 Primary Journey

1. A traveler discovers Greek Essence through search, social media, paid advertising, referral, or direct traffic.
2. The landing page creates emotional relevance and communicates the value proposition.
3. The traveler explores a destination, experience, or sample journey.
4. Trust grows through local expertise, hospitality credibility, process clarity, and useful content.
5. The traveler selects `Plan My Trip`.
6. A multi-step request captures sufficient qualifying information.
7. A confirmation experience explains what happens next.
8. The team receives, acknowledges, owns, and reviews the request.
9. Human follow-up progresses the lead toward consultation, proposal, and booking.

### 14.3 Secondary Journey

A visitor not ready to complete the trip form may:

- book a consultation;
- send an email;
- start a WhatsApp conversation;
- call;
- use Viber if the channel is supported.

These actions must remain visually secondary to the custom-trip request.

### 14.4 Context-Aware Conversion

Every major content page should end with a CTA that connects directly to the topic just explored. Examples:

- `Plan a journey through Paros and Antiparos.`
- `Create a honeymoon around your travel style.`
- `Build the right island-hopping route for your dates.`

### 14.5 Conversion Integrity

The product must not use dark patterns, fake scarcity, misleading pricing, hidden consent, or exaggerated social proof.

### Key Decisions

- One global primary CTA reduces ambiguity.
- Context-aware CTA copy may vary while the core action remains `Plan My Trip`.
