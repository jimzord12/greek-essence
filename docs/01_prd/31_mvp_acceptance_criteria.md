## 31. MVP Acceptance Criteria

The MVP is product-ready when all applicable criteria below are satisfied.

### 31.1 Product Understanding

- A first-time visitor can understand what Greek Essence offers and who it serves within the opening screen and first content section.
- The product clearly communicates tailor-made, authentic, premium travel rather than mass-market booking.
- Journeys are presented as customizable inspiration, not fixed packages.

### 31.2 Conversion

- `Plan My Trip` is consistent and visible across relevant pages.
- The custom-trip request works reliably on mobile and desktop.
- Required data is captured without unnecessary friction.
- The user receives clear confirmation and next-step information.

### 31.3 Operations

- Every successful submission is delivered, recorded, assigned, and acknowledged through a tested workflow.
- Ownership and backup responsibility are documented.
- Failure monitoring and recovery are tested.
- The promised response window is operationally realistic.

### 31.4 Content and Trust

- English and Greek navigation and content behave consistently.
- Four priority destinations contain meaningful, differentiated content.
- At least the approved minimum number of sample journeys is credible and customizable.
- Trust claims are accurate, attributable, permitted, and approved.
- No page depends on placeholder claims, unlicensed media, or misleading evidence.

### 31.5 Quality

- Core navigation and form journeys pass agreed accessibility checks aligned to WCAG 2.2 AA.
- Performance is acceptable on common mobile devices and realistic networks.
- Privacy, cookie, consent, and legal content reflect actual tools and practices.
- Critical pages and actions are measurable.
- No UI implies live booking, availability, pricing, or customer reviews unless true.

### 31.6 Stakeholder Acceptance

- The owner approves the representation of the brand, offer, services, destinations, and trust story.
- The owner is satisfied that the product is intuitive, professionally presented, performant, SEO-ready, and maintainable.

### 31.7 Release Rule

A known failure in submission delivery, acknowledgement, legal compliance, or public truth blocks launch. Minor content or visual imperfections may be prioritized after launch only when they do not materially harm trust, accessibility, comprehension, or conversion.
