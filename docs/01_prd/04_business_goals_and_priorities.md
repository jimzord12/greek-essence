## 4. Business Goals and Priorities

### 4.1 Primary Business Goal

Generate qualified travel-planning leads by encouraging suitable visitors to submit a custom-trip request.

A qualified lead is a traveler whose destination interest, timing, party, budget, preferences, and contact information are sufficient for the Greek Essence team to assess the opportunity and respond meaningfully.

### 4.2 Supporting Business Goals

1. Attract relevant visitors through search, referral, social media, paid campaigns, and direct awareness.
2. Build trust in a new brand through clear positioning, hospitality credibility, transparent process, and high-quality presentation.
3. Encourage meaningful exploration of destinations, experiences, and sample journeys.
4. Establish Greek Essence as a premium, authentic, and personalized alternative to mass-market travel services.
5. Deliver a product that satisfies stakeholder expectations for UI quality, UX, performance, SEO readiness, organization, and maintainability.
6. Establish measurable evidence that informs future content, marketing, operational, and technology investment.

### 4.3 Priority Order

When goals conflict, use this order:

1. Qualified lead generation.
2. Trust and credibility.
3. Relevant traffic acquisition.
4. Useful engagement and inspiration.
5. Stakeholder satisfaction and brand expression.
6. Future extensibility.

Traffic volume alone must not override lead quality or brand positioning.

### 4.4 Stakeholder Success

The owner should regard the MVP as successful when it:

- accurately expresses the intended brand;
- feels unique, polished, intuitive, and trustworthy;
- performs well on common devices and networks;
- has a credible SEO foundation;
- generates actionable evidence and genuine inquiries;
- can be maintained and expanded without disorder.

### Key Decisions

- Qualified intent is more important than raw traffic.
- Engagement is useful only when it contributes to understanding, trust, or conversion.
- Owner satisfaction is an acceptance criterion, not the sole measure of product-market success.
