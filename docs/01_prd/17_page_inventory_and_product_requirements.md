## 17. Page Inventory and Product Requirements

Each page specification defines product purpose, user value, required content, components, conversion role, and acceptance conditions. Final layout and visual behavior belong to the Prototype Specification.

### 17.1 Home

**Purpose:** Establish the brand, communicate the offer, inspire confidence, and move suitable visitors toward exploration and trip planning.

**Primary users:** first-time visitors from search, social, referral, paid media, or direct traffic.

**Primary goals:**

- explain what Greek Essence offers within the opening screen and first content section;
- communicate trust, authenticity, and quiet premium quality;
- introduce priority destinations and experience themes;
- make the planning process understandable;
- drive `Plan My Trip` without forcing conversion too early.

**Required content:**

1. Hero with concise value proposition and primary CTA.
2. Brand promise combining authenticity, personalization, and local expertise.
3. Featured destinations: Athens, Paros & Antiparos, Mykonos, Santorini.
4. Experience themes.
5. How It Works.
6. Sample journeys or route inspiration.
7. Trust and credibility content.
8. Hospitality background or approved relationship evidence.
9. Final context-aware CTA.

**Required components:** C-01, C-02, C-05, C-07, C-11/C-12, C-13, C-14, C-18, C-19, C-23.

**Product rules:**

- avoid too many equally weighted choices above the fold;
- avoid presenting Greek Essence as a booking marketplace;
- the primary value proposition must remain understandable without video or animation;
- trust claims must be approved and attributable.

**Acceptance conditions:**

- a first-time visitor can identify the offer and target audience quickly;
- `Plan My Trip` is obvious but not aggressive;
- priority destinations and planning value are represented;
- the page provides a credible path to deeper content.

### 17.2 Destinations Index

**Purpose:** Help travelers identify which parts of Greece suit their interests, timing, and travel style.

**Required content:**

- editorial introduction;
- visually emphasized priority destinations;
- logically grouped secondary destinations;
- useful traveler-fit or experience cues;
- route or combination inspiration;
- context-aware trip-planning CTA.

**Optional product behavior:** simple thematic filtering by romance, family, culture, gastronomy, relaxation, nightlife, or similar categories if content quality justifies it.

**Rules:**

- no e-commerce-style filtering, price sorting, or availability language;
- destinations must not be represented as interchangeable products;
- priority destinations receive stronger hierarchy than secondary destinations.

### 17.3 Destination Detail

**Purpose:** Explain why a destination matters, who it suits, how it combines with others, and what Greek Essence adds.

**Required content:**

- emotional positioning;
- reasons to visit;
- traveler fit;
- signature experiences;
- local culture and gastronomy;
- accommodation or service style without becoming a hotel catalogue;
- suggested combinations;
- related journey;
- related experiences;
- `Plan a Trip to [Destination]` CTA.

**Required components:** C-04, C-05, C-06, C-08, C-09, C-11/C-12, C-13, C-14/C-15, C-16, C-23.

**Rules:**

- content must be differentiated and locally grounded;
- generic guide content alone is insufficient;
- no claim should imply exclusive access unless accurate and approved.

### 17.4 Experiences Index

**Purpose:** Explain how Greek Essence creates value beyond destination information.

**Required content:**

- tailor-made planning;
- island hopping and complex routing;
- private and authentic local experiences;
- romantic and honeymoon travel;
- family travel;
- culture and gastronomy;
- VIP concierge and travel support;
- what users gain from the planning relationship;
- trip-planning CTA.

**Rules:**

- lead with outcomes and traveler needs;
- operational details must be accurate;
- unconfirmed services must not appear as established offers.

### 17.5 Experience Detail

**Purpose:** Help a visitor understand whether a particular travel style fits them and how Greek Essence can shape it.

**Required content:**

- experience definition;
- intended traveler types;
- possible destinations and combinations;
- representative highlights;
- planning considerations;
- service value;
- related journeys and destinations;
- context-aware CTA.

### 17.6 Journeys Index

**Purpose:** Present credible, customizable examples of what a Greek Essence trip can become.

**Required content:**

- explanation that all journeys are customizable;
- curated journey collection;
- route, duration, traveler type, and mood cues;
- links to relevant destinations and experience themes;
- planning CTA.

**Rules:**

- avoid package-store presentation;
- no fixed pricing or live availability unless operationally supported;
- journey count should remain limited to strong, deliverable examples.

### 17.7 Journey Detail

**Purpose:** Turn inspiration into a concrete route while preserving tailor-made positioning.

**Required content:**

- title, route, duration, traveler type, and trip mood;
- destination sequence;
- selected highlights;
- light day-by-day overview when accurate content exists;
- explanation of customization;
- related destinations and experiences;
- CTA to personalize the journey.

**Rules:**

- clearly distinguish sample structure from guaranteed inclusions;
- avoid false precision where operations are not finalized;
- the page must help the user imagine a journey, not attempt to replace consultation.

### 17.8 About

**Purpose:** Make the new brand human, credible, and differentiated.

**Required content:**

- Greek Essence story and philosophy;
- approved founder or team information;
- local knowledge and international service mindset;
- hospitality background;
- planning philosophy;
- partner network or destination access where approved;
- evidence of experience, awards, or certifications with accurate attribution;
- How It Works summary;
- CTA.

**Rules:**

- do not borrow credibility ambiguously from related businesses;
- avoid vague claims about expertise without context or evidence.

### 17.9 How It Works

**Purpose:** Explain the planning relationship and remove uncertainty.

This may be represented as reusable content on Home, About, and the trip-request journey rather than a dedicated MVP page.

**Expected model:**

1. Tell us how you want to travel.
2. Meet your travel planner.
3. Receive a tailor-made proposal.
4. Refine and prepare for the journey.
5. Travel with support.

The final process and wording must match actual business operations.

### 17.10 FAQ

**Purpose:** Resolve common uncertainty, reduce avoidable contact, and strengthen trust.

**Recommended categories:**

- tailor-made travel;
- planning process;
- planning lead time;
- destination and island selection;
- multi-destination logistics;
- budget expectations;
- family and honeymoon planning;
- support during travel;
- accessibility or special requirements;
- data privacy;
- changes, cancellation, deposits, and payments when approved.

**Rules:**

- publish only accurate answers;
- policy and legal answers require approval;
- unanswered questions should not be filled with generic language.

### 17.11 Contact

**Purpose:** Provide a low-friction path for general questions and secondary communication.

**Required content:**

- short contact form;
- email;
- WhatsApp;
- phone;
- Viber only if actively monitored;
- consultation option;
- service hours and response expectation once defined.

**Rules:**

- do not duplicate the full trip-request form;
- clearly distinguish general contact from custom-trip planning.

### 17.12 Plan My Trip

**Purpose:** Convert a sufficiently informed visitor into a qualified travel-planning lead.

**Required content and behavior:** defined in Section 18.

### 17.13 Legal Pages

**Required pages:**

- Privacy Policy;
- Cookie Information or equivalent consent information;
- Terms or Travel Conditions when finalized.

Legal content requires qualified review and must match actual data handling and commercial practices.

### Key Decisions

- Every page has a distinct product role.
- Page requirements define content and outcomes, not final layouts.
- Major content pages must end with a relevant next step.
