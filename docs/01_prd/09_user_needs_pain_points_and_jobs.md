## 9. User Needs, Pain Points, and Jobs

### 9.1 Core User Needs

Users need:

- a complete, low-stress planning relationship;
- confidence that destinations and accommodation styles fit them;
- access to authentic culture, food, nature, hospitality, and local life;
- reliable transfers, activities, hotels, and partners;
- personalized recommendations instead of generic packages;
- support before and during travel;
- efficient use of time and budget;
- a human expert who listens and can be trusted;
- clarity about the planning process and next steps.

### 9.2 Core Pain Points

Users commonly face:

- uncertainty about which islands or regions suit them;
- difficulty coordinating multi-stop routes and transfers;
- fear of tourist traps, poor fit, or inconsistent quality;
- limited time for research and booking management;
- concern about expensive mistakes;
- uncertainty about balancing iconic destinations with authentic experiences;
- inability to judge whether online recommendations are genuinely local or commercially biased.

### 9.3 Primary Job-to-Be-Done

> Help me design and organize the right trip to Greece for my interests, time, and budget, so I can travel confidently and experience more than the standard tourist route without managing every detail myself.

### 9.4 Supporting Jobs

- Help me decide where to go.
- Show me what a realistic route could look like.
- Help me understand whether the service fits my trip and budget.
- Reassure me that the people behind the service are credible.
- Let me express my preferences without completing an exhausting form.
- Tell me exactly what will happen after I make contact.

### Key Decisions

- Destination selection and logistical uncertainty are central problems, not secondary content topics.
- The product must reduce cognitive load before asking for conversion.
