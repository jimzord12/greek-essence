# Part II — Users and Domain

## 8. Target Audience

### 8.1 Audience Profile

**Age range:** 30–65  
**Primary focus:** 35–55  
**Spending profile:** medium-high to high purchasing power

**Priority markets:**

- United States;
- Canada;
- United Kingdom;
- Australia;
- Germany;
- France;
- Switzerland;
- other affluent Western European markets.

### 8.2 Primary Audience

Affluent international travelers planning a first-time or multi-destination trip to Greece who want expert guidance, convenience, trusted recommendations, and a high-quality experience without investing extensive time in research and coordination.

### 8.3 Audience Segments

| Segment | Primary need | Primary concern | Product response |
|---|---|---|---|
| Couples and honeymooners | A memorable, romantic, highly curated journey | Generic experiences or poor destination choices | Emotional storytelling, romantic routes, privacy, and premium cues |
| Time-poor premium travelers | A seamless personalized plan | Wasted research time or inconsistent service | Clear expertise, process, and planning support |
| Families | Safe, suitable, coordinated travel | Transfers, suitability, comfort, and reliability | Family-fit recommendations and visible logistical competence |
| First-time Greece visitors | Help selecting destinations and routes | Too many options and uncertain logistics | Comparisons, route inspiration, and transparent process |
| Repeat Greece visitors | Experiences beyond familiar landmarks | Repeating conventional tourism | Hidden places, local access, and thematic journeys |
| Luxury-oriented travelers | Quality, discretion, and convenience | Impersonal mass-market treatment | Quiet-premium presentation and curated service proof |

### 8.4 Lower-Priority or Poor-Fit Visitors

The MVP is not primarily designed for:

- travelers choosing solely by lowest price;
- visitors seeking instant availability and self-service booking;
- users looking for standardized last-minute packages;
- audiences requiring services that Greek Essence has not operationally confirmed.

The site should remain respectful and useful, but it should not distort the offer to maximize these audiences.

### Key Decisions

- The product serves international premium travelers, not the entire Greece travel market.
- Copy and information architecture should support first-time and complex-trip planning especially well.
