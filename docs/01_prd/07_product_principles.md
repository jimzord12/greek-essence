## 7. Product Principles

These principles govern trade-offs across content, UX, design, and implementation.

### 7.1 Qualified Intent Over Volume

The product should attract and convert suitable travelers rather than maximize undifferentiated traffic or low-quality inquiries.

### 7.2 Trust Before Conversion

The visitor should understand who Greek Essence is, why it is credible, and what happens next before being asked for significant personal information.

### 7.3 Inspire Before Selling

Place, emotion, and possibility should create desire before the product introduces qualification or operational detail.

### 7.4 Human Expertise Is the Product

Personal listening, judgment, curation, and support are core value propositions. Automation may improve operations later but must not erase the human relationship.

### 7.5 Authenticity Through Specificity

Content should show local knowledge using concrete places, combinations, cultural context, experiences, and operational understanding rather than generic claims.

### 7.6 Quiet Premium

Premium character should come from quality, restraint, confidence, usability, and service clarity—not price-first messaging, decorative excess, or exclusionary language.

### 7.7 Progressive Commitment

The product should request increasing effort only after providing increasing value and confidence. Exploration precedes the trip request; the form reveals manageable steps rather than one long commitment.

### 7.8 One Clear Primary Action

`Plan My Trip` is the dominant global CTA. Secondary channels must remain accessible without competing equally for attention.

### 7.9 Evidence Before Expansion

New destinations, services, integrations, and features should be added when content quality, operational readiness, and validated demand justify them.

### 7.10 Maintainability Over Novelty

The product should prefer clear, stable, reusable patterns over unnecessary complexity, fragile customization, or trend-driven features.

### Key Decisions

- These principles are product-level constraints, not optional design preferences.
- When a feature conflicts with a principle, the conflict must be documented and resolved explicitly.
