## 20. Trust and Credibility Requirements

### 20.1 Trust Objective

Because Greek Essence is a new brand without direct traveler testimonials, the MVP must build confidence through transparent, accurately attributed evidence and process clarity.

### 20.2 Available Trust Sources

Potential sources include:

- hospitality experience;
- founder or team expertise;
- local destination knowledge;
- partner relationships;
- Athenaeum Hotels;
- Porto Bello di Paros;
- Althea Resorts;
- relevant awards, reviews, certifications, or concierge experience where use is approved.

### 20.3 Attribution Rules

Reviews, awards, certifications, or credentials belonging to another entity must never be represented as Greek Essence reviews or awards.

They may be used only when:

- the relationship is explained accurately;
- the claim is directly relevant;
- the rights holder has approved its use;
- the source and owning entity are clear;
- the presentation cannot reasonably mislead the visitor.

### 20.4 Trust Mechanisms

The product should use:

- visible planning process;
- approved team or founder profiles;
- hospitality background;
- verified local relationships;
- transparent support commitments;
- privacy and secure-form reassurance;
- future Greek Essence testimonials and traveler stories;
- clear next steps and response expectations.

### 20.5 Prohibited Trust Practices

- invented testimonials;
- unattributed reviews;
- ambiguous partner logos;
- unsupported superlatives;
- fake counters, bookings, or scarcity;
- misleading claims of exclusivity;
- promises the operational team cannot consistently fulfill.

### Key Decisions

- Trust must be earned through evidence and transparency.
- Related-business credibility may support the story only with explicit attribution.
