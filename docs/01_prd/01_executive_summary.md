# Part I — Product Foundation

## 1. Executive Summary

Greek Essence is a boutique Greece-focused travel-planning brand that creates authentic, premium, tailor-made journeys for international travelers. The product combines local knowledge, trusted hospitality relationships, carefully selected destinations and experiences, and personal planning support.

The first release is an English-first, bilingual marketing and lead-generation website. Its primary purpose is to attract relevant travelers, build sufficient trust and desire, and convert qualified visitors into custom-trip requests. It is **not** an online travel agency, booking engine, package catalogue, or self-service trip platform.

The MVP is also a market-validation product. Before investing in authentication, customer dashboards, itinerary-management systems, payments, automation, or deep third-party integrations, Greek Essence must first demonstrate that it can attract qualified demand and operate a reliable human-led planning workflow.

The product must feel trustworthy, authentic, calm, intuitive, and quietly premium. Visitors should understand that the service may cost more than mass-market alternatives, but that the value lies in avoiding planning mistakes, saving time, gaining local access, receiving personal support, and enjoying a more memorable journey.

### Product Summary

| Area | Definition |
|---|---|
| Product type | Boutique travel-planning marketing and lead-generation website |
| Core offer | Tailor-made journeys in Greece |
| Primary audience | Affluent international travelers, primarily ages 35–55 |
| Primary conversion | Submit a custom-trip request |
| Secondary conversion | Book a consultation |
| Primary value | Trusted, authentic, low-stress Greece travel planning |
| MVP operating model | Human-led planning with manual lead processing |
| Strategic objective | Validate demand while establishing a maintainable foundation for future growth |

### Key Decisions

- Qualified custom-trip requests are the product's primary outcome.
- The MVP validates demand before major operational software investment.
- Human expertise is part of the product, not an implementation limitation.
- Quiet premium quality, trust, and authenticity are more important than visual novelty or feature volume.
