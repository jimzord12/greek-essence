## 5. Success Model and Metrics

### 5.1 Measurement Philosophy

The MVP begins without reliable historical benchmarks. Initial targets are therefore hypotheses, not promises or universal industry standards. The first 8–12 weeks of meaningful traffic should establish a baseline, after which targets should be revised using actual acquisition mix, lead quality, device usage, seasonality, and operational capacity.

### 5.2 North-Star Outcome

**Qualified custom-trip requests per month.**

This number should always be interpreted together with lead quality and downstream business outcomes.

### 5.3 Conversion Funnel

The product should measure:

1. Relevant visitor arrives.
2. Visitor views meaningful destination, experience, journey, or trust content.
3. Visitor clicks `Plan My Trip`.
4. Visitor starts the trip-request form.
5. Visitor progresses through the form.
6. Visitor submits a complete request.
7. The request is accepted as qualified.
8. A consultation or substantive follow-up occurs.
9. A proposal is created.
10. The proposal becomes a booking.

### 5.4 Provisional MVP Targets

These ranges are internal starting hypotheses and must be replaced by observed baselines:

| Metric | Provisional target |
|---|---:|
| Visitor-to-qualified-lead conversion, sitewide | 1–3% |
| Primary CTA click-through from high-intent content | 5–12% |
| Trip-form start-to-submission completion | 40–65% |
| Successful submission delivery and acknowledgement | 100% |
| Meaningful lead response within promised service window | 95%+ |
| Core analytics event coverage | 100% of defined funnel events |

A 50% visitor-to-lead rate is not an appropriate sitewide expectation. It may occur only in highly qualified referral or campaign traffic and should not be used as the general product target.

### 5.5 Engagement Indicators

Useful supporting indicators include:

- engaged sessions;
- average engagement time on priority pages;
- pages viewed per engaged session;
- destination and journey progression;
- return visits;
- mobile versus desktop behavior;
- language usage;
- CTA interaction by page type;
- form abandonment by step and field;
- acquisition source quality.

### 5.6 Downstream Business Indicators

Initially tracked manually:

- lead-to-consultation rate;
- consultation-to-proposal rate;
- proposal-to-booking rate;
- average expected trip value;
- reasons for lost or rejected leads;
- response time;
- operational workload per lead.

### Key Decisions

- Numeric targets are provisional until sufficient real traffic exists.
- Funnel quality matters more than isolated vanity metrics.
- Submission reliability and response ownership are non-negotiable.
