## 19. Content Requirements

### 19.1 Language Strategy

English is the primary launch language; Greek is secondary.

English must be written for an international audience rather than mechanically translated from Greek. Greek content must also be professionally localized. Both languages must represent the same product truth, even where phrasing and examples differ.

### 19.2 Messaging Hierarchy

1. **Emotional promise:** Experience Greece beyond the ordinary.
2. **Practical value:** Tailor-made planning without avoidable stress or wasted time.
3. **Reason to believe:** Local knowledge, hospitality experience, and trusted relationships.
4. **Service model:** Personal guidance before and during the trip, according to the confirmed offer.
5. **Action:** Tell us how you want to travel.

### 19.3 Content Principles

Content must:

- sell the feeling and meaning of the journey, not only the destination name;
- replace clichés with specific, sensory, locally grounded detail;
- use concise pages with strong hierarchy;
- avoid unsupported claims such as `best service`;
- explain the planning process clearly;
- provide value before conversion;
- avoid price-led language unless a deliberate qualification strategy is approved;
- distinguish confirmed facts, examples, and future possibilities;
- remain maintainable in both languages.

### 19.4 MVP Content Inventory

Required:

- homepage copy;
- brand story and About content;
- confirmed service descriptions;
- four priority destination pages;
- curated secondary-destination overview;
- three to six operationally credible sample journeys;
- planning-process copy;
- FAQ answers;
- form labels, helper text, errors, confirmation, and emails;
- contact and support information;
- approved trust claims and attribution;
- English and Greek metadata;
- privacy, cookie, and applicable travel terms.

### 19.5 Content Governance

Before publication, every claim must have an owner and approval path. English and Greek updates should use a shared content model or controlled process to reduce drift.

### 19.6 Media Requirements at Product Level

The MVP requires high-quality, properly licensed images that communicate genuine place, people, culture, food, craft, landscape, and boutique hospitality. The specific art direction belongs to the Prototype Specification.

Original photography and video are a future strategic priority because they strengthen differentiation, trust, SEO, social media, and paid campaigns.

### Key Decisions

- Localization is product work, not a final translation task.
- Content quality and truth take precedence over page count.
- No unlicensed or misleading media may be used.
