## 12. Destination Priorities

### 12.1 Priority Destinations

1. **Athens** — gateway to Greece and strong beginning or ending point for multi-destination routes.
2. **Paros & Antiparos** — strategic priority due to local presence and partner network; positioned around refined Cycladic character, beaches, villages, gastronomy, hospitality, relaxation, couples, and honeymoon travel.
3. **Mykonos** — premium hospitality, nightlife, international appeal, and high-end experiences.
4. **Santorini** — iconic scenery, romance, honeymoons, and once-in-a-lifetime appeal.

### 12.2 Secondary Destinations

- Naxos;
- Milos;
- Syros;
- Crete;
- Peloponnese, including Corinthia, Nafplio, Mani, Monemvasia, and Ancient Olympia;
- Meteora;
- Delphi.

### 12.3 Launch Content Strategy

The MVP should prioritize four strong, differentiated destination pages rather than many thin pages. Secondary destinations may initially appear in a curated `More of Greece` overview until sufficient original, useful, and search-worthy content exists.

### 12.4 Destination Content Requirements

Every destination detail page must answer:

- Why is this destination special?
- Which traveler types does it suit?
- What can Greek Essence unlock there?
- How can it combine with other destinations?
- Which authentic experiences are relevant?
- What planning risk or complexity does Greek Essence reduce?

### Key Decisions

- Content depth is more important than destination count at launch.
- Paros and Antiparos receive strategic emphasis.
