# Appendix A — Product Decision Records

## DEC-001 — Primary Product Goal

**Decision:** The website's primary goal is generating qualified custom-trip requests.

**Rationale:** Greek Essence sells a human-led planning service. A completed request is the clearest indication of relevant intent and creates the information needed for follow-up.

**Implications:** Traffic, design, content, and analytics must support qualification and conversion rather than maximize page views alone.

---

## DEC-002 — MVP as Market Validation

**Decision:** The first release validates demand before major travel-platform investment.

**Rationale:** Authentication, booking, payment, customer portals, and itinerary automation introduce substantial cost and complexity before demand and workflow are proven.

**Implications:** The MVP uses a reliable human-led operational process and measures downstream outcomes.

---

## DEC-003 — Tailor-Made Positioning

**Decision:** Greek Essence is positioned as a boutique tailor-made travel partner, not a package seller.

**Rationale:** Personalization, local knowledge, and human judgment are the primary differentiators.

**Implications:** Use `Journeys` or `Sample Itineraries`; avoid transactional package-store patterns.

---

## DEC-004 — Primary CTA

**Decision:** `Plan My Trip` is the consistent global primary CTA.

**Rationale:** One recognizable action reduces decision friction and reinforces the service model.

**Implications:** Other contact methods remain available but visually secondary.

---

## DEC-005 — Trust as Primary Emotional Goal

**Decision:** Trust is the most important emotional outcome, followed by authenticity and quiet premium quality.

**Rationale:** Travelers are considering a meaningful financial and emotional investment with a new brand.

**Implications:** Evidence, clarity, attribution, process, and reliability take precedence over decorative novelty.

---

## DEC-006 — Quiet Premium

**Decision:** Premium character is expressed through quality and restraint rather than ostentation.

**Rationale:** The intended audience values expertise, comfort, and authenticity without loud status signaling.

**Implications:** Avoid discount language, clutter, aggressive selling, and excessive visual effects.

---

## DEC-007 — Four Priority Destinations

**Decision:** Athens, Paros & Antiparos, Mykonos, and Santorini receive dedicated launch emphasis.

**Rationale:** These destinations combine market recognition, route relevance, romantic and premium appeal, and existing local or partner strength.

**Implications:** Secondary destinations begin in a grouped overview until strong content exists.

---

## DEC-008 — Multi-Step Qualification

**Decision:** The custom-trip request uses a concise multi-step structure.

**Rationale:** The business needs meaningful qualification data, but a single long form would create avoidable friction.

**Implications:** Preserve data, show progress, explain budget, and measure step abandonment.

---

## DEC-009 — Human Expertise Remains Visible

**Decision:** Human planning and support are presented as product value, not hidden behind automation.

**Rationale:** Listening, judgment, local knowledge, and service are central differentiators.

**Implications:** Future automation should improve operations without making the experience generic or impersonal.

---

## DEC-010 — Bilingual MVP

**Decision:** The MVP supports English as primary and Greek as secondary.

**Rationale:** The target market is international while the business also requires a Greek presence.

**Implications:** Localization governance, equivalent routes, metadata, and content maintenance are required.

---

## DEC-011 — No Booking, Payment, or Accounts in MVP

**Decision:** Transactional and authenticated capabilities are excluded.

**Rationale:** They do not directly support the initial validation goal and significantly increase complexity and obligations.

**Implications:** The UI must not imply these capabilities.

---

## DEC-012 — Trust Asset Attribution

**Decision:** Related-hospitality credentials may be used only with explicit, accurate attribution and permission.

**Rationale:** Ambiguous presentation would mislead visitors and create legal and reputational risk.

**Implications:** Every claim requires an approved source and presentation.

---

## DEC-013 — Evidence-Based Expansion

**Decision:** Future features and content expansion require validated need and operational readiness.

**Rationale:** Premature breadth creates thin content, technical debt, and distracted execution.

**Implications:** Post-launch analytics and business outcomes inform priorities.

---

## DEC-014 — Maintainability Over Novelty

**Decision:** The project favors organized, mature, battle-tested patterns over novelty.

**Rationale:** The product should become more stable as it grows rather than harder to change.

**Implications:** Specific package and framework choices must be justified in the Technical Design using current evidence.

---
