## 25. Prototype Definition of Done

The prototype is ready for client review when:

- the agreed stack, file structure, local content validation, and locale routes are implemented;
- all PRD-required major page types are represented with the Prototype’s approved hierarchy;
- English and Greek navigation, content relationships, missing-translation behavior, canonicals, sitemap, and language switch work;
- C-01–C-30 have the required visible representative states or a documented prototype reason for absence;
- the custom four-step form supports contextual prefilling, conditional child ages, Back/Continue, localStorage draft persistence for 72 hours, manual clearing, validation focus, safe retry, and no value loss;
- a controlled submit sends one readable lead notification to the configured Gmail inbox and one acknowledgement to a controlled traveller test address through Resend;
- success appears only after both email sends are accepted; a simulated failure preserves draft data;
- no secret, form value, or real customer data is exposed in browser code, URLs, Git, logs, or presentation captures;
- Home, a detail page, navigation, Contact, and all form steps work at 390 px and 1440 px, remain usable at 320 px and 200% text zoom, and honor reduced motion;
- critical Playwright, build, lint, type, content, metadata, accessibility-smoke, and performance checks pass;
- all public claims/media are approved or clearly presented as prototype placeholders;
- §22 is reviewed with the owner so the prototype is not mistaken for the production commercial MVP.
