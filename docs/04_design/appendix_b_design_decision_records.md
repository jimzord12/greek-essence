# Appendix B — Design Decision Records

## DDR-001 — Editorial Typography

**Decision:** Use Cormorant Garamond for display roles and Inter for body/interface roles.

**Reason:** The pairing balances boutique editorial warmth with high interface readability and supports the English/Greek bilingual requirement.

**Constraint:** Keep Cormorant out of dense UI and body copy.

---

## DDR-002 — Aegean Editorial Palette

**Decision:** Use deep Aegean blue, muted teal, warm ivory, limestone, slate, sand, and limited olive.

**Reason:** The palette expresses place through depth and natural materials without resorting to bright tourism blue or stereotypical white-and-blue branding.

---

## DDR-003 — Light Appearance Only

**Decision:** The prototype supports light mode only.

**Reason:** Mediterranean light, photography, prototype scope, and maintainability outweigh the marginal value of dark mode.

---

## DDR-004 — Soft Editorial Geometry

**Decision:** Use 10 px controls, 16 px cards, and 20 px selected large media/dialog radii.

**Reason:** This provides warmth and modernity without producing playful or SaaS-like bubble UI.

---

## DDR-005 — Minimal Natural Elevation

**Decision:** Use whitespace, surface, typography, and borders before shadows.

**Reason:** The product should feel architectural and editorial rather than layered like an application dashboard.

---

## DDR-006 — Cinematic Editorial Heroes

**Decision:** Use large art-directed photography with functional scrims and restrained overlays.

**Reason:** Inspiration is a product principle, but comprehension and accessibility remain mandatory.

---

## DDR-007 — Subtle Mediterranean Decoration

**Decision:** Allow a limited line-based motif vocabulary.

**Reason:** Pure editorial restraint benefits from a small identifiable craft layer, provided it remains non-stereotypical and subordinate.

---

## DDR-008 — Semantic Token Ownership

**Decision:** Components and pages use semantic tokens rather than raw values.

**Reason:** This makes the visual system maintainable and allows controlled refinement after owner review.

---

## DDR-009 — Form Calmness

**Decision:** The form uses generous spacing, persistent labels, strong focus, quiet surfaces, and clear error hierarchy.

**Reason:** The form is the primary conversion product and must feel respectful rather than administrative.

---

## DDR-010 — Accessibility as Premium Quality

**Decision:** Contrast, focus, reflow, reduced motion, and bilingual readability are visual acceptance gates.

**Reason:** Trust and quality fail when the interface is difficult to perceive or operate.

---
