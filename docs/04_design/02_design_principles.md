## 2. Design Principles

### 2.1 Editorial clarity

Typography, imagery, composition, and whitespace establish hierarchy. Decorative UI chrome remains secondary.

### 2.2 Mediterranean light, not postcard blue

The visual identity draws from sea depth, limestone, sun-warmed plaster, muted vegetation, and natural shadow. It avoids bright tourism blue, stark white-and-blue clichés, and oversaturated imagery.

### 2.3 Warm restraint

The design should feel welcoming without becoming playful. Warmth comes from ivory surfaces, photography, human content, display typography, and measured roundedness.

### 2.4 Photography carries emotion

The interface should not compete with imagery using gradients, glass effects, large illustrations, decorative backgrounds, or overbuilt cards.

### 2.5 Visual truth

Design must never imply a product capability or trust signal that does not exist. A beautiful false implication is still a product failure.

### 2.6 Accessibility is visual quality

Contrast, focus, readable type, stable layout, target size, and reflow are part of the premium experience, not compliance decoration.

### 2.7 Greek and English are equal design citizens

Every token, component, and page composition must work with Greek text, longer labels, different word shapes, and language-specific wrapping.

### 2.8 Purposeful variation

Not every section should look identical, but every variation must derive from tokens and a defined editorial purpose.
