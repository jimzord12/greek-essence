## 3. Design Anti-Patterns

The following approaches are prohibited unless this document is revised and approved:

- glassmorphism or frosted-glass surfaces;
- neumorphism;
- large decorative gradients;
- metallic gold as a luxury shortcut;
- excessive teal or bright cyan accents;
- bright “Greek flag” blue-and-white branding;
- recurring Greek-key/meander patterns;
- ancient columns, amphorae, laurel crowns, or mythology motifs as UI decoration;
- Santorini silhouette graphics;
- heavy paper, stone, or watercolor textures;
- oversized display type that reduces comprehension;
- text placed over uncontrolled photography without a contrast strategy;
- dense price/package cards;
- floating cards over every image;
- frequent drop shadows;
- multiple equally strong CTA colors;
- pill styling for every control;
- rounded “bubble UI”;
- auto-advancing carousels;
- scroll-jacking, heavy parallax, elaborate page transitions, or decorative loaders;
- inconsistent radii or arbitrary spacing;
- icon-only actions without clear accessible names;
- decorative illustrations that imitate trust credentials;
- dark mode in the prototype;
- “luxury” expressed through exclusionary copy, glossy effects, or status symbolism.
