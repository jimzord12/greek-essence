## 17. Decorative Mediterranean Details

The interface may use a small vocabulary of subtle Mediterranean details.

### 17.1 Approved motifs

- fine editorial divider ornaments;
- thin geometric separators influenced by Cycladic architecture;
- abstract coastline or wave-line forms;
- restrained framing around selected quotes;
- limited botanical line art where contextually relevant;
- subtle route-line details.

### 17.2 Constraints

- Decoration must not appear in every section.
- One page should generally use no more than one recurring motif family.
- Decorative lines should use sand, teal, or low-contrast neutral tokens.
- Decorative SVGs must be lightweight, scalable, and hidden from assistive technology.
- Motifs must not reduce text contrast or create visual clutter.
- Decorative elements must never imitate certification, partner, map, or trust marks.

### 17.3 Prohibited motifs

- recurring Greek key/meander;
- classical columns and pediments;
- laurel crowns;
- amphorae and mythology;
- bright wave graphics;
- tourist-map illustration;
- postcard silhouettes;
- faux hand-painted textures.
