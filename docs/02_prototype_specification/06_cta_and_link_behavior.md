## 6. CTA and Link Behavior

### 6.1 Hierarchy

| Level | Use | Typical label |
|---|---|---|
| Primary | Begin custom-trip request | `Plan My Trip` |
| Secondary | Consultation or meaningful exploration | `Book a Consultation`, `Explore Paros & Antiparos` |
| Tertiary | Related content, back, or supporting detail | `View journey`, `Learn how it works` |
| Contact | Direct channel with channel identity | `Message on WhatsApp`, `Call us` |

Only one primary-styled action should dominate a viewport or section. When two actions coexist, their hierarchy must remain obvious.

### 6.2 Context and continuity

- Content-page CTAs may use contextual headings and reassurance, but the primary button remains `Plan My Trip`.
- Activating a CTA from a destination, experience, or journey should carry that source context into the request experience and preselect or display it where appropriate; the user can edit it.
- External consultation or communication actions must clearly indicate what will happen. Do not unexpectedly open an app without a recognizable channel label.
- Links use descriptive text, have visible hover/focus states, and do not rely on color alone.

### 6.3 Action states

All actionable controls specify default, hover where applicable, focus-visible, pressed, disabled, loading where applicable, and error/success feedback. Disabled controls must not be used as the sole explanation of what is required.
