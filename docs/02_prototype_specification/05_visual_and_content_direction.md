## 5. Visual and Content Direction

### 5.1 Design character

The interface should feel trustworthy, authentic, premium boutique, clean, timeless, calm, editorial, and image-led. It should feel Greek through light, landscape, material, hospitality, food, craft, and cultural specificity—not decorative stereotypes.

### 5.2 Provisional visual-system direction

Pending formal brand approval, high-fidelity exploration may use:

- a restrained nature- and Aegean-inspired palette derived from the existing blue/aqua identity;
- warm neutral surfaces to avoid a cold corporate feel;
- an editorial display face for selected headings paired with a highly readable interface/text face;
- minimal iconography with a consistent stroke and optical character;
- roundedness, borders, and shadows used sparingly and consistently;
- one unambiguous primary action treatment and quieter secondary/tertiary styles.

Exact color, typography, spacing, and logo rules remain provisional design-system decisions.

### 5.3 Content hierarchy

Each page should generally communicate:

1. Page identity and emotional proposition.
2. Practical relevance or traveler fit.
3. Specific evidence, examples, or route possibilities.
4. Greek Essence planning value.
5. Trust and next-step reassurance.
6. Contextual conversion.

Copy should be concise, specific, internationally understandable, and professionally localized. Avoid unsupported superlatives, vague exclusivity, clichés, and price-first framing.

### 5.4 Photography

Photography must:

- create a genuine sense of place;
- balance landscapes with people, culture, food, craft, architecture, and boutique hospitality;
- show candid, natural moments and varied traveler ages/party types;
- use natural light and restrained processing;
- be properly licensed and accurately depict the named place or experience;
- avoid generic stock staging, excessive saturation, empty luxury symbolism, and repetitive postcard views.

Hero media requires a safe focal area across aspect ratios. Cropping must preserve the subject and editorial meaning. Text overlays require a tested contrast treatment independent of the image. Meaningful images receive useful alternative text; decorative images use no redundant announcement. Galleries include captions or location context where useful.

Video is optional. It must never block comprehension, autoplay with sound, replace the value proposition, or become required for navigation. A visible pause control is required for motion that persists.

### 5.5 Iconography and maps

- Icons support labels; they do not replace unfamiliar text.
- Route visuals communicate sequence and relative relationship, not exact geography unless explicitly designed as maps.
- Decorative Greek motifs should be rare, abstracted, and subordinate to content.

### 5.6 Trust presentation

Trust should be distributed through the journey rather than isolated in one logo wall:

- opening language sets a precise promise;
- process sections make the relationship predictable;
- founder/team content gives accountable human presence;
- destination and journey copy demonstrates expertise;
- approved hospitality relationships and credentials show source, owner, and relationship clearly;
- form and contact surfaces explain privacy, next steps, and response expectations when approved.

Do not style editorial quotes as testimonials. Do not show star ratings, traveler counts, award marks, partner logos, or review excerpts without approved evidence and attribution.
