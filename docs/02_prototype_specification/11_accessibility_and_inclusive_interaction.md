## 11. Accessibility and Inclusive Interaction

The prototype targets WCAG 2.2 AA at the UX and interaction level.

### 11.1 Structure and navigation

- One clear H1 per page and logical heading order.
- Landmarks for header/navigation, main content, and footer.
- A skip-to-main-content action appears on keyboard focus.
- Keyboard focus order follows reading and visual order.
- Visible focus has sufficient contrast and is never removed.
- Repeated navigation is consistent across pages and languages.

### 11.2 Perception and readability

- Text and essential controls meet AA contrast; text over photography is tested against every crop/state.
- Body text is comfortably readable and remains usable at 200% text zoom.
- Information does not depend on color, position, motion, sound, or imagery alone.
- Alternative text describes purpose and context, not visual decoration already expressed nearby.
- Captions/transcripts are required for meaningful prerecorded media.

### 11.3 Controls and targets

- Controls have clear names, roles, states, and predictable outcomes.
- Pointer targets aim for 44 × 44 CSS pixels and avoid tightly packed actions.
- Hover content is also available through focus/activation and can be dismissed.
- Gestures have single-pointer alternatives; swiping is never the only way to operate a gallery.

### 11.4 Language and localization

- Page language and language changes are communicated.
- Greek and English are tested for reflow, pronunciation cues where needed, and non-truncated labels.
- Dates, phone examples, names, and contact expectations are clear for international users.
- Copy avoids idioms that translate poorly or presume travel expertise.

### 11.5 Cognitive load

- Use plain instructions and consistent labels.
- Keep choices curated and grouped.
- Preserve form progress and provide error recovery.
- Avoid auto-advancing content, unexpected context changes, and urgency patterns.
- Critical information such as customization, budget rationale, and next steps appears at the point of decision.
