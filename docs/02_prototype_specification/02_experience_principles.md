## 2. Experience Principles

### 2.1 Trusted host, not catalogue

The experience should resemble guidance from a cultivated Greek host: warm, specific, calm, internationally aware, and capable. Editorial explanation precedes collections; expert judgment is visible; choices are curated rather than exhaustive.

### 2.2 Inspire before requesting commitment

Photography, destination character, authentic experiences, and believable route ideas create desire first. Process clarity and trust then reduce risk. The trip request appears as the natural next step, not an interruption.

### 2.3 Reduce uncertainty at every stage

Pages answer fit-oriented questions: why this place, who it suits, how it combines, what complexity Greek Essence removes, and what happens next. Labels, links, and CTAs describe outcomes rather than vague exploration.

### 2.4 Quiet premium

Premium character comes from restraint, strong composition, generous space, excellent imagery, clear language, careful detail, and predictable behavior. Avoid dense chrome, promotional devices, excessive ornament, loud luxury codes, and novelty interactions.

### 2.5 Authenticity through specificity

Specific places, routes, culture, gastronomy, craft, seasonal context, and operational knowledge must replace generic claims and tourism clichés. Visuals must show real place and human texture rather than postcard stereotypes alone.

### 2.6 Human expertise remains visible

Team presence, planning steps, contextual recommendations, and contact options make the service relationship tangible. Automation must not dominate the perceived experience.

### 2.7 Progressive commitment

The interface asks for more effort only after giving more value and clarity. Forms use concise steps, optionality is explicit, and users understand why sensitive qualification information such as budget is requested.

### 2.8 One primary action

`Plan My Trip` is the persistent global CTA label. Contextual headings may vary, but the button label should remain consistent. Consultation and direct-contact options must not receive equal visual weight.

### 2.9 Honest representation

The interface must never suggest scarcity, live prices, availability, bookings, customer reviews, awards, partnerships, or exclusive access unless those statements are true, approved, and correctly attributed.
