## 7. Page Specifications

All major pages use the following fields: purpose, user goal, product goal, required content and hierarchy, actions, variants/states, responsive/accessibility behavior, interaction notes, acceptance criteria, and PRD traceability.

### 7.1 Home

**Purpose:** Establish the offer, inspire trust and desire, and route suitable first-time visitors toward discovery and planning.  
**User goal:** Understand what Greek Essence does, whether it suits the trip, and where to explore next.  
**Product goal:** Communicate tailor-made positioning and generate qualified intent without premature pressure.

**Required hierarchy:**

1. Global header.
2. Immersive hero: brand promise, concise practical value, `Plan My Trip`, optional secondary exploration link.
3. Brand promise: authenticity, personalization, local knowledge, and reduced planning burden.
4. Featured destinations: Athens, Paros & Antiparos, Mykonos, Santorini; Paros & Antiparos may receive strategic emphasis without making the others feel incidental.
5. Experience themes: outcome-led discovery.
6. How It Works: concise planning relationship.
7. Sample journeys: credible route inspiration and customization statement.
8. Trust story: people, hospitality background, approved relationships/credentials.
9. Final contextual CTA with reassurance.
10. Global footer.

**Primary action:** `Plan My Trip`.  
**Secondary actions:** Explore Destinations, Journeys, or How It Works; consultation remains quieter.  
**Variants/states:** Hero may use still image or approved non-blocking video; journey module has populated and unavailable variants.  
**Responsive behavior:** Hero media crops around a defined focal area; collections reflow from four/three columns to two and one; How It Works becomes a vertical sequence; no horizontal carousel is required for comprehension.  
**Accessibility:** Value proposition remains readable without media; section headings form a logical outline; any video has controls and equivalent message in text.  
**Interaction notes:** Optional gentle section reveals must not delay access. Featured card relationships are editorial, not filter-like.  
**Acceptance criteria:** Within the opening screen and first section, a first-time visitor can identify Greece, tailor-made planning, human/local expertise, intended premium audience, and the primary next step. The page cannot be mistaken for a booking or package marketplace.  
**Related PRD:** §§1, 6–7, 13–17.1, 19–20; FR-003–008, FR-016; NFR-011–012.

### 7.2 Destinations Index

**Purpose:** Help travelers decide which parts of Greece fit their interests, timing, and travel style.  
**User goal:** Compare curated possibilities and find a relevant destination or combination.  
**Product goal:** Demonstrate destination judgment and route visitors to differentiated detail content.

**Required hierarchy:**

1. Header and editorial hero/introduction.
2. Orientation cues explaining how to choose.
3. Featured priority collection: Athens, Paros & Antiparos, Mykonos, Santorini.
4. Route/combination inspiration.
5. `More of Greece` secondary collection, logically grouped.
6. Optional simple theme filters only when content coverage supports meaningful results.
7. Contextual CTA and footer.

**Primary action:** Open a destination; final `Plan My Trip`.  
**Secondary actions:** Explore related experiences or journeys.  
**Variants/states:** No-filter default; filtered results; no matching results with reset and guidance; unavailable secondary content.  
**Responsive behavior:** Priority hierarchy remains evident at all widths through ordering, scale, or editorial feature treatment. Optional filters wrap as labeled controls and never resemble commerce facets.  
**Accessibility:** Filter state is announced, controls are keyboard accessible, and results update does not unexpectedly move focus.  
**Interaction notes:** Filters must be minimal (for example romance, family, culture, gastronomy, relaxation, nightlife) and additive only if useful; no price, availability, or sort-by-commerce controls.  
**Acceptance criteria:** Users distinguish priority from secondary destinations, identify traveler-fit cues, and find at least one combination path.  
**Related PRD:** §§12, 15, 17.2; C-11–C-12, C-15–C-16; FR-003, FR-016.

### 7.3 Destination Detail

**Purpose:** Explain a destination’s character, fit, combinations, and the value Greek Essence adds.  
**User goal:** Decide whether the destination belongs in their trip and understand what a well-planned visit might feel like.  
**Product goal:** Convert destination interest into deeper exploration or a contextual trip request.

**Required hierarchy:**

1. Breadcrumbs.
2. Destination hero: name, precise emotional positioning, location cue, relevant image, contextual CTA.
3. Editorial introduction.
4. Why visit / defining character.
5. Who it suits: traveler types, pace, mood, relevant considerations.
6. Signature experiences.
7. Local culture and gastronomy rich-story section.
8. Accommodation and service style, without hotel listings.
9. Suggested destination combinations with route summary.
10. Related sample journey.
11. Related experiences and destinations.
12. `Plan a Trip to [Destination]` CTA block with `Plan My Trip` button.
13. Footer.

**Primary action:** `Plan My Trip`, carrying destination context.  
**Secondary actions:** View related journey, experience, or combination destination.  
**Variants/states:** Priority destination; secondary destination detail when content matures; gallery with/without captions; journey unavailable fallback.  
**Responsive behavior:** Split editorial sections stack semantically; galleries use a compact grid or controlled viewer; route sequence becomes vertical.  
**Accessibility:** Place names and image alternatives are accurate; gallery navigation is labeled; itinerary relationships are expressed in text as well as visually.  
**Interaction notes:** A gallery viewer, if used, supports close, previous/next, keyboard control, focus containment, and returns focus to its trigger.  
**Acceptance criteria:** The page answers why it is special, who it suits, what Greek Essence can add, how it combines, and what planning complexity is reduced. It contains no unsupported access or exclusivity claims.  
**Related PRD:** §§10.4, 12, 17.3; C-04–C-16, C-23; FR-003, FR-007, FR-016.

### 7.4 More of Greece

**Purpose:** Present secondary destinations honestly without creating thin standalone pages.  
**User goal:** Discover less prominent options and understand where they might fit.  
**Product goal:** Show breadth while protecting content quality and priority-destination focus.

**Required hierarchy:** editorial introduction; grouped destination entries for Naxos, Milos, Syros, Crete, Peloponnese, and Meteora & Delphi; concise fit and combination cues; related journeys where available; planning CTA.  
**Primary action:** Explore an available secondary destination or `Plan My Trip`.  
**States:** Full entry, summary-only entry, and content-unavailable state. Summary entries must not imitate complete detail pages.  
**Responsive/accessibility:** Anchored navigation may become a scrollable labeled index; headings must make every region directly navigable.  
**Acceptance criteria:** Secondary content feels curated, useful, and subordinate to the four launch priorities; no destination is padded with generic copy.  
**Related PRD:** §§12.2–12.4, 15.2, 17.2; NFR-012.

### 7.5 Experiences Index

**Purpose:** Explain Greek Essence’s value by traveler need and way of traveling.  
**User goal:** Recognize an experience style that fits and understand the planning help provided.  
**Product goal:** Differentiate the service from generic destination content.

**Required hierarchy:**

1. Editorial hero and explanation of experience-led planning.
2. Tailor-Made Greece as foundational offer.
3. Island Hopping and complex routing.
4. Private/authentic local experiences within confirmed themes.
5. Honeymoons & Romantic Travel.
6. Family Travel.
7. Culture & Gastronomy.
8. VIP Concierge & Travel Planning.
9. Service benefit grid: time, coherence, trusted partners, personalization, support.
10. How It Works summary.
11. Contextual planning CTA.

**Primary action:** Open an experience; final `Plan My Trip`.  
**Secondary actions:** Related destinations and journeys.  
**Variants/states:** Card collection or alternating editorial features; unconfirmed themes are omitted, not shown as `coming soon`.  
**Responsive/accessibility:** Ordering—not only card size—communicates importance; icons never replace labels.  
**Acceptance criteria:** Users understand outcomes, fit, planning complexity removed, and human-service value. The page does not promote group tours, hiking, cruises, or MICE without approval.  
**Related PRD:** §§11, 17.4; C-13, C-18, C-21, C-23; FR-004, FR-006–007.

### 7.6 Experience Detail

**Purpose:** Help visitors evaluate a travel style and see how Greek Essence can shape it.  
**User goal:** Understand what the experience means, whether it suits the party, and which destinations/routes support it.  
**Product goal:** Turn thematic interest into qualified intent.

**Required hierarchy:** breadcrumbs; experience hero and definition; intended traveler types; representative outcomes/highlights; destinations and combinations; planning considerations; Greek Essence service value; related journey; related destinations/experiences; contextual CTA.  
**Primary action:** `Plan My Trip`, carrying experience context.  
**Secondary actions:** Explore related destination or journey.  
**Variants/states:** Each confirmed experience theme; unavailable related content.  
**Responsive/accessibility:** Highlights reflow to a linear narrative; planning considerations remain visible rather than hidden behind hover.  
**Acceptance criteria:** The experience is benefit-led, accurate, and distinguishable from a bookable product or guaranteed inclusion.  
**Related PRD:** §§10.5, 11, 17.5; FR-004, FR-007, FR-016.

### 7.7 Journeys Index

**Purpose:** Make tailor-made possibilities concrete through credible example routes.  
**User goal:** Find an itinerary shape matching duration, party, and mood.  
**Product goal:** Build confidence in route expertise and encourage personalization.

**Required hierarchy:**

1. Editorial hero and explicit `Every journey is customizable` statement.
2. Curated collection of three to six approved journeys.
3. Optional guidance by duration, traveler type, or mood when the collection supports it.
4. Multi-destination planning value.
5. Related destination/experience pathways.
6. Planning CTA.

**Primary action:** View a journey; final `Plan My Trip`.  
**Secondary actions:** Explore destinations or experiences.  
**Variants/states:** Populated collection; limited collection; no approved journey state with destination and planning alternatives.  
**Responsive/accessibility:** Journey metadata stays adjacent to titles; routes are text-readable; card heights need not be artificially equal if that harms content.  
**Interaction notes:** No live pricing, availability, package filters, or misleading booking language.  
**Acceptance criteria:** Every card shows title, route, duration, traveler/mood cue, image, and customization statement.  
**Related PRD:** §§10.3, 17.6, 19.4; C-14–C-16; FR-005, FR-016; NFR-011.

### 7.8 Journey Detail

**Purpose:** Turn inspiration into a believable route while preserving tailor-made positioning.  
**User goal:** Imagine the flow, highlights, and suitability of a journey and decide whether to personalize it.  
**Product goal:** Demonstrate planning competence and generate contextual requests.

**Required hierarchy:**

1. Breadcrumbs.
2. Hero: journey title, route, duration, traveler type, mood, representative image.
3. Prominent customizable-sample notice.
4. Editorial overview and traveler fit.
5. Route summary with destination sequence and suggested allocation where approved.
6. Selected highlights.
7. Light day-by-day overview only when accurate content exists.
8. What can be customized.
9. Planning notes and explicit boundary between example and guaranteed inclusion.
10. Related destinations and experiences.
11. `Personalize this journey` contextual heading with `Plan My Trip` button.

**Primary action:** `Plan My Trip`, carrying journey context.  
**Secondary actions:** Explore route destinations or related experiences.  
**Variants/states:** With/without day-by-day content; different route lengths; unavailable journey state retaining useful alternatives.  
**Responsive behavior:** Horizontal route becomes vertical; day sections use headings or an accessible optional accordion; sticky summary is permitted on wide viewports but not required.  
**Accessibility:** Sequence is conveyed with ordered text, not only a map; accordions retain heading semantics and independent controls.  
**Acceptance criteria:** A user can explain the route, duration, fit, highlights, and customization status without assuming price, availability, or guaranteed inclusions.  
**Related PRD:** §§10.3, 17.7; C-09, C-14–C-16, C-23; FR-005, FR-007; NFR-011.

### 7.9 About

**Purpose:** Make the new brand human, credible, and differentiated.  
**User goal:** Understand who is responsible, what experience they bring, and why their advice is trustworthy.  
**Product goal:** Reduce perceived risk without borrowing or inventing credibility.

**Required hierarchy:** brand-story hero; philosophy and promise; founder/team profiles; local knowledge plus international service mindset; hospitality background; approved network/relationship evidence; planning philosophy; accurately attributed credentials; How It Works summary; CTA.  
**Primary action:** `Plan My Trip`.  
**Secondary actions:** How It Works, Contact, consultation.  
**Variants/states:** Founder-led or team-led profile; relationship content hidden until approved; credentials with explicit source/owner.  
**Responsive/accessibility:** Portraits remain associated with names and roles; logo/credential meaning is also stated in text.  
**Acceptance criteria:** Users can identify accountable people and relevant expertise. No related-business review or award can reasonably be interpreted as belonging to Greek Essence.  
**Related PRD:** §§6, 17.8, 20; C-17–C-20, C-23; FR-006; NFR-011–012.

### 7.10 How It Works pattern / optional page

**Purpose:** Make the human planning relationship predictable.  
**User goal:** Know the effort, sequence, people involved, and immediate next step.  
**Required hierarchy:** short introduction; five steps—share preferences, meet planner, receive proposal, refine/prepare, travel with approved support; reassurance and caveats; CTA.  
**Variants:** Compact 3-step summary may be used on Home; full 5-step version on About, optional page, and request introduction. Compact wording must not contradict the full process.  
**Responsive/accessibility:** Ordered vertical presentation on compact; numbering and labels remain textual.  
**Acceptance criteria:** Process wording matches actual operations before high-fidelity approval; unknown support or timing is marked provisional.  
**Related PRD:** §§10.2, 17.9, 18.6–18.7; C-18; FR-006.

### 7.11 FAQ

**Purpose:** Resolve recurring uncertainty and strengthen trust with approved answers.  
**User goal:** Find a clear answer without unnecessary contact.  
**Product goal:** Remove objections and reduce avoidable operational work.

**Required hierarchy:** editorial intro; optional category index; approved questions grouped by tailor-made travel, process, timing, destinations/logistics, budget, family/honeymoon, support, accessibility/special requirements, privacy, and approved commercial policies; unresolved-question contact route; CTA.  
**Primary action:** Expand/read answer; final `Plan My Trip`.  
**Secondary actions:** Contact for an unanswered question.  
**Variants/states:** Accordion or visible list; category with no approved answers is omitted. Search is not required for MVP.  
**Responsive/accessibility:** Each accordion trigger is a button within a heading, exposes expanded state, controls its panel, and is independently keyboard accessible. Opening one item must not close another unless users retain control and the behavior is made clear.  
**Acceptance criteria:** Policy answers are approved; focus is stable; answers remain available without pointer or animation; no generic filler answers.  
**Related PRD:** §17.10; C-22; FR-006, FR-008.

### 7.12 Contact

**Purpose:** Provide low-friction help for questions that do not require full trip qualification.  
**User goal:** Choose a suitable monitored channel and understand when/how the team will respond.  
**Product goal:** Capture general inquiries while steering trip-planning intent toward the primary form.

**Required hierarchy:** clear distinction between general contact and trip planning; short contact form; monitored contact options; consultation option; service hours and response expectation when approved; privacy notice; trip-planning CTA; footer.  
**Short-form fields:** name, email, reason/topic, message, privacy acknowledgement; phone only if operationally justified.  
**Primary action:** Send general inquiry within this page; `Plan My Trip` remains the site-wide primary conversion and is clearly offered for itinerary requests.  
**Secondary actions:** Email, WhatsApp, phone, Viber if approved, consultation.  
**States:** Initial, field error, submitting, submission failure with retry, success with next step, channel unavailable.  
**Responsive/accessibility:** Form precedes or follows contact methods based on tested priority; input types and labels suit mobile; direct actions include channel and destination context.  
**Acceptance criteria:** The form does not duplicate trip qualification; only monitored channels appear; failures preserve input and do not show false success.  
**Related PRD:** §17.11; C-24, C-26, C-28–C-29; FR-008, FR-012, FR-015.

### 7.13 Plan My Trip / Request a Custom Itinerary

**Purpose:** Convert informed visitors into qualified custom-trip requests.  
**User goal:** Share enough information to receive meaningful human follow-up without facing an exhausting or opaque form.  
**Product goal:** Capture usable qualification data reliably and respectfully.

**Required hierarchy:** minimal header with escape route and language access; title `Request a Custom Itinerary`; concise value/effort explanation; trust and privacy reassurance; progress indicator; current step heading and fields; Back/Continue or Submit controls; secondary save/recovery guidance only if supported; help/contact route; footer or compact legal links; confirmation after success.  
**Primary action:** Continue, then `Send My Request` or similarly explicit submit label. Global `Plan My Trip` is not repeated as a competing action inside the form.  
**Secondary actions:** Back; exit to relevant content; contact for help; optional consultation after confirmation.  
**Variants/states:** New request, context-prefilled request, each of four steps, field/group error, step summary error, submitting, recoverable submission failure, success confirmation, session/data unavailable.  
**Responsive/accessibility:** Detailed in §9.  
**Acceptance criteria:** Detailed in §9 and §15.  
**Related PRD:** §§14, 17.12, 18; C-25, C-27–C-29; FR-007, FR-009–012, FR-015.

### 7.14 Submission Confirmation

**Purpose:** Confirm receipt, reduce uncertainty, and keep the visitor oriented.  
**User goal:** Know that the request succeeded, what happens next, when to expect contact, and how to correct information.  
**Required hierarchy:** unambiguous success heading; non-sensitive request reference if available; next-step summary; approved response window or honest statement that timing will be confirmed; correction/addition method; optional consultation action; relevant inspiration; main navigation route.  
**Primary action:** Context-dependent next step; consultation remains optional.  
**States:** Success only after confirmed receipt; acknowledgement-email pending must not invalidate on-screen success; partial or unknown delivery is an error, not success.  
**Accessibility:** Focus moves to the confirmation heading; status is announced once without trapping focus; no information relies only on an icon.  
**Acceptance criteria:** User can state what happens next and how to make a correction. The page is not a dead end.  
**Related PRD:** §§18.6–18.7; C-27; FR-010–011.

### 7.15 Legal Pages

**Purpose:** Provide understandable access to approved privacy, cookie, and travel terms.  
**User goal:** Find, navigate, and understand applicable policies.  
**Required hierarchy:** breadcrumbs; page title; effective/updated date if approved; concise summary where legally permitted; table of contents for long documents; approved legal body; contact route; footer.  
**Actions:** In-page navigation and relevant contact; no conversion interruption inside legal text.  
**States:** Final approved content; unavailable Terms page is omitted rather than filled with placeholder law.  
**Responsive/accessibility:** Readable measure, descriptive headings, visible focus, accessible tables/lists, and anchor offset below sticky header.  
**Acceptance criteria:** All applicable policies are reachable from every page footer and data-collection point; prototype text is visibly marked as non-final internally.  
**Related PRD:** §§17.13, 19–20; FR-012, FR-017; NFR-004.

### 7.16 Not-found and content-unavailable pages

**Purpose:** Recover orientation without misleading the visitor.  
**Required content:** clear explanation, Home link, relevant index links, `Plan My Trip` only when contextually appropriate, and contact for genuine support.  
**Variants:** Page not found; translation unavailable; journey withdrawn/unavailable; content collection empty.  
**Rules:** Never invent replacement content or silently redirect to an unrelated page. Preserve the current language where possible.  
**Acceptance criteria:** Users understand what failed and have at least one safe recovery route.  
**Related PRD:** C-29–C-30; FR-015; NFR-002.
