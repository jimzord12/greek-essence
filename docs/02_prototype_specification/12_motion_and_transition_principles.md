## 12. Motion and Transition Principles

- Motion supports orientation, hierarchy, and feedback; it is never required to understand content.
- Preferred effects are short fades, restrained transforms, gentle image changes, and direct state transitions.
- Avoid heavy parallax, scroll-jacking, decorative loaders, continuous background movement, auto-advancing carousels, and elaborate page transitions.
- Navigation and accordion motion should feel immediate; users must not wait for animation before acting.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences by removing non-essential movement and using instant or cross-fade state changes.
- Persistent video/motion provides pause control.
- Motion must not cause focus to move unpredictably or content to reflow after the user targets an action.
