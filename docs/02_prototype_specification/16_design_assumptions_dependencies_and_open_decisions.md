## 16. Design Assumptions, Dependencies, and Open Decisions

### 16.1 Provisional design assumptions

1. Three responsive modes are sufficient for prototype coverage, with intermediate-width inspection.
2. How It Works is primarily reusable and does not require a dedicated MVP route unless content or testing shows a need.
3. Secondary destinations initially live in a curated More of Greece experience.
4. Contextual CTAs may prefill a visible, editable source interest in the form.
5. The form begins immediately with Step 1 after concise orientation rather than adding an intro step.
6. FAQ uses an accessible accordion at compact widths and may use an open list at wider widths.
7. A still image is the safe default hero treatment; video remains optional.
8. Exact minute-based form effort is omitted until measured.
9. No optional filters are required unless the available content produces useful, non-empty distinctions.
10. No sticky mobile CTA is assumed until prototype testing confirms it helps without obstructing content.

### 16.2 Design and content dependencies

- Final logo and usage rules.
- Approved palette, typography, icon, spacing, and photography direction.
- Licensed/original image library and focal/caption metadata.
- Final English copy and professional Greek localization.
- Approved founder/team biographies and portraits.
- Confirmed service list and support promise.
- Three to six operationally credible journeys.
- Approved FAQs and legal/commercial answers.
- Approved relationship, award, review, certification, and logo permissions.
- Final contact channels, owners, hours, consultation path, and response promise.
- Budget strategy, ranges, currency presentation, and international-flight treatment.
- Privacy, cookie, consent, retention, deletion, and travel terms.

### 16.3 Open decisions requiring owner or specialist approval

| Decision | Prototype treatment until resolved | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Active launch services beyond confirmed core | Omit from primary IA and cards | Product owner / operations |
| Three to six launch journeys | Use clearly marked realistic provisional examples | Product owner / operations / content |
| Qualifying budget and whether flights are included | Use non-numeric placeholder ranges and explicit annotation | Product owner / commercial |
| Response-time commitment | Use `We will explain the next step` without a numeric promise | Operations |
| Lead owner, backup, and tracking destination | Model user-visible success/failure only; block launch acceptance | Operations |
| Consultation process/tool | Use a labeled prototype action without prescribing mechanism | Product owner / operations |
| Viber retention | Show only in an annotated optional contact variant | Operations |
| Public relationship wording | Use attribution component shell; no public factual claim | Product owner / rights holders |
| Awards, reviews, certifications, and logos | Omit until permission and attribution are approved | Product owner / legal |
| Final visual identity | Use provisional art direction and design tokens | Product owner / brand design |
| Image availability/licensing budget | Use licensed representative placeholders with asset status | Product owner / content |
| Legal and travel terms | Use structural wireframe only; no invented policy language | Legal / product owner |
| Greek localization ownership | Demonstrate representative localized screens; flag full coverage dependency | Product owner / content |
| Dedicated How It Works route | Keep reusable; validate through IA/testing | Product/design |
| Optional destination filters | Omit by default; add only with sufficient content evidence | Product/design/content |

No designer, developer, or AI contributor may settle public claims, commercial obligations, data handling, or operational commitments through visual design alone.
