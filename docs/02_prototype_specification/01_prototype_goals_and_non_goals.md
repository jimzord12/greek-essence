## 1. Prototype Goals and Non-Goals

### 1.1 Goals

The prototype must demonstrate that:

1. A first-time visitor quickly understands Greek Essence as a boutique, human-led, tailor-made Greece travel-planning service.
2. Visitors can discover destinations, experience themes, and customizable journeys without mistaking the site for a booking marketplace.
3. Trust is established through people, process, specificity, hospitality experience, and accurately attributed evidence.
4. `Plan My Trip` remains the single dominant action while secondary contact remains accessible.
5. The four-step trip request feels manageable, respectful, useful, and easy on a phone.
6. English and Greek experiences remain structurally equivalent and preserve context.
7. Major page types and components work coherently across compact, medium, and wide viewports.
8. A usability test can evaluate comprehension, discovery, trust, form completion, and recovery from errors.
9. The design creates a stable basis for a visual system and later implementation handoff.

### 1.2 Non-goals

The prototype must not imply or simulate as real:

- live availability, booking, payment, or price comparison;
- fixed packages or guaranteed itinerary inclusions;
- user accounts, dashboards, or itinerary management;
- real-time inventory or partner systems;
- unapproved testimonials, awards, exclusivity, support levels, response times, or commercial policies;
- a large editorial publishing system;
- unconfirmed services as primary offers.
