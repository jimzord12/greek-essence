## 15. Prototype Acceptance Criteria

The first high-fidelity clickable prototype is ready for stakeholder review and usability testing when all applicable criteria below pass.

### 15.1 Information architecture

- All PRD-required MVP page types are represented.
- Primary navigation order and Contact/footer placement match the PRD.
- Priority and secondary destinations have distinct hierarchy.
- Destinations, experiences, and journeys have meaningful internal relationships.
- Legal and language routes are discoverable.

### 15.2 Page composition

- Every major page has a clear H1, purpose, content hierarchy, and relevant next step.
- Home contains all required sections in a coherent inspiration-to-trust-to-conversion sequence.
- Detail pages answer fit, specificity, planning value, relationship, and next-step questions.
- Journey pages clearly state customization and do not imply price or availability.
- About and trust content use transparent attribution patterns.

### 15.3 Components and states

- C-01 through C-30 have a represented design or a documented reason they are not visible in the chosen prototype dataset.
- Primary controls show default, focus, pressed, disabled where relevant, loading, error, and success states.
- Empty, unavailable, media-failure, translation, form-error, submission-error, and confirmation patterns are represented.
- Component semantics and CTA hierarchy remain consistent across page types.

### 15.4 Responsive behavior

- Core screens exist at 390 px, 834 px, and 1440 px reference widths.
- Home, one content detail, navigation, Contact, and all form steps are fully designed at compact and wide modes.
- Layout remains usable at 320 px and 200% text zoom.
- No essential content/action depends on hover or causes horizontal viewport scrolling.
- Mobile menu, route sequence, galleries, cards, and form controls have explicit reflow behavior.

### 15.5 Form and conversion

- `Plan My Trip` is consistent across relevant entry points.
- Source context is visibly carried into and editable within the form.
- All four steps and conditional fields are represented.
- Back/Continue preserves data.
- Validation identifies and focuses errors without clearing valid input.
- Submission failure preserves data and offers safe retry.
- Success is distinct from failure and explains next steps, timing when approved, corrections, and optional consultation.

### 15.6 Accessibility

- Keyboard paths, focus order, menu containment/return, accordion operation, gallery operation, form labels/errors, and confirmation focus are annotated and testable.
- Color contrast, focus visibility, heading structure, target sizing, alt-text intent, reduced motion, and zoom/reflow have been reviewed against WCAG 2.2 AA expectations.
- English and Greek text do not truncate critical meaning.
- Information is never color-, image-, motion-, or pointer-dependent.

### 15.7 Brand and truth

- The prototype feels calm, authentic, trustworthy, and quietly premium without relying on visual excess.
- Placeholder content is internally marked and cannot be mistaken during handoff for approved business truth.
- No unapproved service, relationship, award, review, response time, budget threshold, legal term, or exclusive access is asserted as fact.
- No screen implies live booking, payment, availability, pricing, or customer accounts.

### 15.8 Test readiness and handoff

- Flows A–H are clickable without facilitator intervention except where a deliberate error is simulated.
- Prototype annotations identify content dependencies, conditional logic, responsive changes, focus behavior, and provisional decisions.
- Usability tasks can be completed with realistic content rather than lorem ipsum.
- Deviations from this specification or the PRD are recorded and approved rather than silently introduced.
